LIBRARY RULES

Libraries are serene environments dedicated to focused reading, learning, and academic enrichment, making it crucial to maintain silence at all times. Loud conversations, ringing phones, or any kind of disturbance should be strictly avoided to ensure a respectful atmosphere for fellow readers. Mobile phones must be kept on silent, and personal devices should be used discreetly. Borrowed books must be returned within the due date to avoid fines, and utmost care must be taken to prevent damageâ€”writing, folding pages, or tearing is not allowed. While individual study is encouraged throughout the library, group study is permitted only in designated collaborative zones. Library computers and internet access are provided strictly for educational, research, or professional use, and browsing inappropriate content or accessing personal social media accounts is discouraged. Users should refrain from bringing food and beverages into the premises to maintain cleanliness and protect library materials. Photography, videography, or photocopying requires prior approval from the library staff. Additionally, users are expected to carry valid ID cards while using library facilities, maintain proper seating etiquette, and follow any specific instructions provided by librarians or posted notices. Adhering to these rules ensures that the library remains a welcoming, efficient, and fair space for all users.
